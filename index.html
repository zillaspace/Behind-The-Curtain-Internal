Where Things Stand

The plan so far

Architect engaged

Dave Rybak of Rybak Architecture and Development is the recommended architect, selected over Willis Pember and Charles Cunniffe for his city relationships, value engineering, EPIC permit knowledge, and a prior no cost review of the Isis. Schematic design and renderings are underway.

Cost to permit

Phases 1, 3, and 4 carry the project to permit submittal: design $289,364, structural $57,000, MEP $31,740, theatre $10,000, fire suppression $12,000, with a preliminary contractor pricing component to establish an accurate construction budget. Total through permit, about $400,104, running June to October 2026.

Lead gift framework

A $5,000,000 lead gift, inclusive of an immediate $500,000 to fund design through permitting, released against documented project milestones. Naming above the Isis double doors is tied to the personal lead gift only, with interior lobby signage as the documented fallback if the City or Historic Preservation does not approve the exterior sign.

Funds the lead donor raises from others are recognized through other named spaces on the gift chart, decided gift by gift. Recognition never conveys design authority, which rests with the board and architect. This is the short answer to how additional donors are recognized, worth having ready.

Presentation Strategy

How to sell it

01

Lead with legacy

Start every conversation with preserving a living piece of Aspen history, not with square footage.

02

Match donor identity to space

A filmmaker belongs in a screening room. A writer belongs in the café. A family foundation belongs over the doors. A social host belongs at the bar.

03

Create scarcity

Use purchased opportunities and reserved spaces to create momentum. Scarcity is essential in a market like Aspen.

04

Make recognition beautiful

Bronze, stone, leather, glass, archival photography, warm light. Recognition should feel like a private club, not a municipal plaque board.

Suggested House Gesture

The Permanent Toast

A signature, non seasonal drink that lives with the theatre forever.
